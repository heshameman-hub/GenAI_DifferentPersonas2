Here are three different creative uses for paperclips:

1. **The Emergency Zipper Pull or Garment Repair:** A paperclip can be straightened, bent into a simple hook shape, and used to replace a lost or broken zipper pull on a coat or backpack. More creatively, multiple paperclips linked together can serve as temporary "stitches" or a safety pin replacement to quickly mend a torn hem, a broken bra strap, or a dangling button in a pinch.

2. **The Micro-Sculpting or Model-Making Armature:** Paperclips, particularly the larger jumbo sizes, are made of malleable steel wire that can be easily bent and shaped. They can be used as the internal framework (armature) for very small, delicate sculptures made of clay or epoxy, providing structural stability to tiny limbs or antennae. They can also be straightened and filed to become miniature flagpoles, railing supports, or precise antenna arrays for scale models (like trains or miniature war games).
